1. Cranberries

Cranberries have been identified as an excellent dietary source of high-quality antioxidants, which have been shown to help maintain healthy cognitive functioning.

2. Oats

Oats supply energy to the brain, which may improve learning. They are also rich in selenium, an antioxidant that has been shown to have protective effects in brain disorders and age-related cognitive problems

3. Almonds

Almonds are one of the most concentrated sources of vitamin E available, and vitamin E at a high daily dose has been shown to delay the progression of Alzheimer's disease.
